Wisdom Teeth Removal in Dallas – Gentle & Safe Extraction

Impacted or problematic wisdom teeth can cause pain and crowding. Our experienced team removes them comfortably so you can protect your oral health.

Why Remove Wisdom Teeth?

Wisdom teeth are the last set of molars to erupt, often in your late teens or early twenties. Because there’s usually not enough space for them, they can grow sideways, become impacted or push against neighbouring teeth. The Cleveland Clinic notes that extraction is recommended when wisdom teeth are crooked, impacted, causing pain, trapping food, contributing to gum disease or tooth decay, or when a cyst forms around the tooth. Removing problematic wisdom teeth prevents pain, infection and long‑term damage to your smile.

Benefits of Wisdom Teeth Removal

Proactively removing problematic wisdom teeth can reduce your risk of gum disease, tooth decay, damage to neighbouring teeth, bone loss and jaw damage. Extraction also alleviates pressure and discomfort, making it easier to maintain good oral hygiene and prevent future complications. While full recovery takes about one to two weeks, most people return to work or school within three to five days.

The Procedure & Sedation Options

Before the extraction, we evaluate your teeth with digital X‑rays and discuss your sedation options. On the day of the procedure we numb the area completely and can provide nitrous oxide or oral conscious sedation if you prefer. The tooth is gently removed, and stitches may be placed to aid healing. Most extractions take less than an hour, and you’ll be able to go home once you’re comfortable.

Recovery Tips

After surgery you’ll rest while the anaesthetic wears off. Keep gauze over the extraction site for the first 30 minutes to control bleeding and avoid rinsing, spitting or using straws so you don’t dislodge the clot. Rest for the first three to five days and limit physical activity; swelling and soreness are normal, and cold compresses and over‑the‑counter pain relievers help. Eat soft foods and avoid hard, crunchy or spicy items. Do not smoke or consume carbonated or alcoholic beverages for the first few days. Gently clean the area with mouthwash or salt water without swishing, and brush and floss your other teeth carefully. Following these guidelines reduces the risk of dry socket and promotes comfortable healing.
